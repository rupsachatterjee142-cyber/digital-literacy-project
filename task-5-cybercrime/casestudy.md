Case Study: UPI "Request Money" Fraud

1. Nature of the Crime
It is a social engineering crime, common in India, where scammers take advantage of the victim’s lack of digital literacy in terms of the way UPI application works. The scammers aim to deceive the victim into voluntarily authorizing a fund transfer from their own bank accounts.
2. How the Crime Occurs (Step-by-Step)
The Hook: The scammer will either contact the victim through an online marketplace, e.g., OLX, or pose as a bank official. They will show interest in "buying" the item or will tell the victim that they have won a "cash back" prize.

The Deception: Instead of paying the victim, the scammer will send the victim a "Request Money" notification using a UPI application (e.g., GPay, PhonePe, etc.). They will tell the victim, "I have initiated the payment; you just need to enter your UPI PIN to claim the money."

The Theft: In reality, the victim will need to enter their UPI PIN only if they need to send money, not if they need to receive it. When the victim enters their PIN, they will inadvertently allow the scammer to debit their account. The money will instantly be transferred from the victim’s account to the scammer’s account.

The Exit: After the scammer is successful, they will immediately block the victim. They will transfer the money through different "mule" accounts to prevent detection.
3. Consequences
The victim suffers an instant financial loss. Since the transaction is technically "authorized" by the user's secret PIN, it is often hard for banks to reverse the transaction, making it an extremely effective scam.
